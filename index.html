Non-native vs Native Speakers

Select a language origin to filter the graph

English and Mandarin stand out as the two most widely spoken languages globally. A nuanced insight emerges from the analysis of this graph, revealing a profound contrast in the demographic characteristics of those proficient in these languages. Notably, over half of the world's English speakers have acquired it as a secondary language, distinct from their mother tongue. In stark contrast, almost 90% of Mandarin speakers are native speakers. This data underscores the remarkable prevalence of English as the most widely learned second language worldwide.

What about Australia and the English language?

This map offers a comprehensive overview of English proficiency on a global scale. Notably, regions such as Australia and the United States exhibit a significant concentration of English speakers, in line with expectations. However, it also unveils intriguing insights, such as Iceland, a country where Icelandic is the official language, boasting an impressive 98% population with fluency in English. This data underscores the widespread influence and adaptability of the English language.
